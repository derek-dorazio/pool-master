# Hero Image Prompt — 01 Season Ticket

**Save output as:** `brand/concepts/01-season-ticket/hero.jpg`
**Aspect ratio:** 4:5 portrait (e.g. 1200×1500 or 1024×1280)
**Target mood:** timeless, heritage, almanac, quiet confidence — *"the pool we've run for twenty years"*

---

## Prompt (paste into Midjourney / DALL-E 3 / Gemini / Nano Banana)

> A moody still-life photograph, shot on a dark walnut wood table: a vintage sports almanac open to a handwritten pool bracket, a weathered leather-bound ledger, a brass fountain pen resting on an aged cream paper scorecard, a folded felt pennant in forest green and gold with the letters "PM", a cup of black coffee with rising steam, soft golden-hour window light from the upper left casting long warm shadows. Editorial magazine photography, shallow depth of field, film grain, ektachrome palette leaning into deep forest green, cream paper, antique gold, oxblood burgundy accents. Cinematic, quietly masculine, heritage aesthetic — think Wes Anderson meets a 1970s sports annual. No text overlays. No modern devices.

**Midjourney tail:** ` --ar 4:5 --style raw --stylize 300`
**DALL-E 3 note:** request "portrait orientation"; remove the MJ tail.

---

## Negative prompt / avoid

- No phones, laptops, screens, TVs, modern electronics
- No team logos from real leagues (NFL/NBA/MLB)
- No text readable in the image (captions render in HTML over this)
- No fluorescent or cold lighting
- No plastic / synthetic surfaces
- Avoid over-saturation; keep the palette warm and slightly muted

## Alt direction if the first is too busy

> A clean overhead flat-lay on aged cream paper: a handwritten playoff bracket filled out in fountain-pen ink, a folded forest-green felt pennant with gold trim, a brass paperclip, a single die. Warm side-lit natural light, film grain, generous negative space in the upper third so copy sits cleanly. Editorial, minimal, heritage.

## How the hero slot uses this

The hero sits on the left ~550px-wide pane. The headline *"Where your league lives."* overlays the upper third. Keep the **upper third and lower-left quadrant** relatively uncluttered so copy remains legible. A dark overlay is applied in CSS regardless.
