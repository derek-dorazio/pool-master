## Prompt (paste into Midjourney / DALL-E 3 / Gemini / Nano Banana)

> A cinematic photograph of a single neon sign glowing against a wet midnight-navy concrete wall at 2am. The sign reads "DRAFT NIGHT" in clean geometric neon tubes — **electric cyan** for the primary letters, with a **royal electric-blue** halo rather than a secondary color. The sign is understated and architectural, not party-neon. A shallow pool of rainwater below reflects the cyan glow as a mirrored streak. Shot on 35mm film, Kodak Portra, long exposure, shallow depth of field, very clean composition, film grain, subtle chromatic aberration. The atmosphere is cold, still, and quiet — no crowd, no chaos. Think Blade Runner 2049's color script, the stillness of a Roger Deakins frame, with the precision of a pro-sports broadcast graphic. Palette: midnight navy, electric cyan, royal electric blue, frost-white highlights — no warm tones whatsoever. No people. No other readable text beyond "DRAFT NIGHT."

**Midjourney tail:** ` --ar 4:5 --style raw --stylize 400`
**DALL-E 3 note:** request "portrait orientation, cinematic monochromatic blue neon photography."

## Negative prompt / avoid

- **No warm tones** — no pink, magenta, orange, red, amber, yellow, or green. This palette is strictly navy / cyan / royal blue / frost-white.
- No people, no faces, no figures
- No corporate sports logos
- No text other than "DRAFT NIGHT" on the sign
- No party/dive-bar clutter — this is the pro-sleek sibling of the Neon variant
- Avoid CGI/game-engine look — film photography realism
- No excessive fog or haze that mutes the glow

## Alt direction — architectural glass

> An abstract composition: a slab of frost-tinted glass etched with the word "DRAFT NIGHT" in thin geometric type, backlit by a single electric-cyan LED bar, sitting on polished concrete. Royal-blue shadows, rain on the concrete, film grain. Architectural, minimal, quiet.

## How the hero slot uses this

The hero sits on the left ~620px-wide pane. Headline *"Draft day, every day."* renders frost-white with a cyan-glowed accent word over the upper third. Place the neon sign **right half or lower third** so copy overlay has breathing room. Keep the overall contrast **higher** than the Neon variant — this direction relies on crisp edges and glass-clean reflections, not soft bokeh chaos.
